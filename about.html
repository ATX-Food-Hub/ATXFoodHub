About ATX Food Hub

ATX Food Hub is a student-driven initiative focused on making food access resources visible, accessible, and community-driven. Through mapping, research, outreach, and peer support, we are tackling food insecurity head-on.

🔧 How We Work

  • Vice President: Oversees growth, ensures coordination, and leads general meetings (6–7 hrs/week)
  • Committee Leads: Coordinate committee work, set deadlines, and update leadership (4–5 hrs/week)
  • Committee Members: Make project contributions, attend meetings, and support initiatives (2–3 hrs/week)

🧠 Our Committees

📣 Outreach & Engagement

We amplify awareness and ensure our map and resources reach those who need them. Projects include a new community garden, pantry anti-theft pilots, and SNAP peer support. Members also create social media content, table at events, and give presentations.

🔬 Research

This team studies how our resources are used, what works, and how to improve them through qualitative and quantitative data—partnering with UT faculty and presenting at conferences.

🗺️ Map Improvement

Our developers and designers make the map easier to use and more informative by adding transit routes, chatbots, tutorials, and improving layout. Ideal for creative thinkers and coders.

💡 What You’ll Gain

  • Real-world experience in outreach, research, and digital mapping
  • Leadership, communication, and project implementation skills
  • Connections to local orgs and UT faculty
  • Opportunities to publish and present work
